Don't ever cover more than 35 numbers in Roulette! Some or even ALL of your bets may end up being worthless!

What is "Bet Nullification"?

Some people really like to spread their bets around the Roulette table. They don't just make one or two bets covering a small section of the table, they go hogwild, taking up a ton of real estate on the felt. They're hedging their bets to increase their chances of getting paid, even though it results in a smaller overall payout.

But if you end up spreading yourself too thin, you can put yourself in a situation where it becomes IMPOSSIBLE to win. This is easy to see if you bet boths sides of an even money bet (When you bet on 18 numbers at a time).

For example, you could bet $100 on Red AND $100 on Black and \(36 \over 38\) (94.7%) of the time you'll end up with no gain and no loss. In this case, when the Roulette ball lands on any number between and , your $100 winning bet will cancel out your $100 losing bet.

But when you hit the or the , you'll lose ALL $200!

This is a result of Bet Nullification (I made this term up). Whenever the win from one bet completely cancels out the losses of another bet or set of bets, there is zero upside to making the bets at all.

And creating a Bet Nullification situation is a mathematical certainty once you hit the threshold of betting on 36 different numbers.

The Red/Black bet example is an EXTREMELY simple example of how you could make bets that nullify each other, but are there bet combinations that are more difficult to identify?

Protect Yourself

So if you ever find yourself betting on 36 different numbers, you should know that you've triggered a Bet Nullification situation and that you should try to identify the nullified bets so that you can remove them from the table. You'll save yourself a lot of EV with zero downside!
