If you're the type of person who likes to spread their bets around the Roulette table, you should know about the Distributive Property of Roulette Betting. It can save you a lot of time, hassle, and awkwardness if your bets can be consolidated into larger multi-number bets.

What is the Distributive Property of Roulette Betting?

The Distributive Property of Roulette Betting states

Betting $X on an n-number bet is equivalent to betting \($X \over n\) each of the n-numbers Straight-up.

Which can be represented formulaically as

\({$X \times Payout_n} = n \times {$X \over n} \times Payout_1 \)

Where \(\color{#8acdd8}Payout_n\) is the payout multiplier for an n-number bet (when \(n \neq 5\)).

So betting $6 on the 6-number "Double Street" is equivalent to making six $1 bets on each of the covered numbers individually.

Does this really work?

If we look at the two sets of wagers above, we can easily see that hitting any number outside of - will result in a $6 loss. But what happens when we hit a number between - ?

With the single $6 Double Street bet, we win 5-to-1, for a net win of $30.

With the six $1 Straight-up bets, we will lose $5, but win $35 on the Straight-up bet, for a net win of $30.

So in both scenarios, we lose $6 when we miss and win $30 when we hit.

And this Distributive Property of Roulette Bets works with any combination of bets where the bets are all uniform. So you can consolidate three Straight-up bets into a 3-number Street bet. And you can consolidate four Straight-up bets into one 4-number Corner bet. You can even consolidate two Street bets into a single Double Street bet.

And this works for all bets with the exception of the 5 number (Basket) bet, which is a bet on the numbers , , , , and .

What is this Sorcery?

This all magically works out because all Roulette Bets (besides that pesky 5-number bet) have the same house edge (5.26%!). This means if you bet the same amount on n spots, your Expected Value (EV) should be exactly the same if you bet that same amount on and n-number bet.

And we can prove this mathematcially using one of the formulas I've introduced in my post on the Basics of Roulette Math. The payout for every bet in Roulette (besides the 5 number "Basket" bet) can be calculated by using the formula:

$$ { Payout_n } = { {36 - n} \over n } $$

So an $n wager on an n-number bet can be calculated as

\begin{align} n \times { Payout_n } & = { n \times { {36 - n} \over n } } \\ \\ & = 36 - n \\ \\ & = 36 - 1 - n + 1 \\ \\ & = { {36 - 1} \over 1 } - (n - 1) \\ \\ & = { Payout_1 } - (n - 1) \\ \end{align}

Which is the win from a single $1 Straight-up bet minus the (n-1) losses from the losing Straight-up bets.

For example, if you bet $1 on 12 numbers Straight-up, then one of those bets will win $35 and 11 of those bets will lose, for a net win of $24, which is what your 2-to-1 payout would be for betting $12 on a dozen or column.

\begin{align} $12 \times { Payout_{12} } & = { Payout_1 } - (12 - 1) \\ \\ $12 \times 2 & = $35 - $11 \\ \\ $24 & = $24 \\ \end{align}

Terminology

If you've never heard of the Distributive Property of Roulette Betting, it's probably because I just made up the name. I've recently been analyzing some Roulette betting systems and realized how a lot of them waste time by making you sprinkle bets needlessly all over the layout. I've never seen this property formally discussed anywhere, so I thought I'd give it a name.

So, with that being said, let me also introduce you to some of the other terms involved in this property that you may have already figured out.

Consolidation

Consolidation is "rolling-up" individual bets into a single bet with the same win/loss characteristics. A typical consolidation is taking n number of $X bets into a single $Nx n-number bet.

Distribution/Decomposition

Distribution or Decomposition is taking a multi-number bet and breaking it down into smaller bets, often a series of "Straight-up" single-number bets.

It's also possible to decompose a multi-number bet into smaller multi-number bets. For example, a $12 double-street bet can be broken down into two $6 street bets. Or it could be broken down into one $6 street bet and three $2 Straight-up bets. Or it could be broken down into an $8 Corner bet and a $4 Split bet.

Density

Density or Betting Density is the bet amount per spot once a multi-number bet has been fully distributed to single-number bets. You can also think of this as the equity per spot.

In order for two betting scenarios to be equivalent, their betting density must be equal for the covered area. For example, you can bet $6 on the - Double Street, but that would have a different density compared to betting $3 on the , , , Corner Bet in conjunction with a $3 , Split Bet. This implies that the two betting schemes are not equivalent and will pay out differently when the numbers hit.

How can I make use of the Distributive Property of Roulette Betting?

I've been watching a lot of Roulette system reviews on CEG Dealer School's YouTube Channel, and I've noticed that the systems submittied by viewers are often impractical when it comes to betting at a brick and mortar casino.

These systems may seem enjoyable on paper or even tapping your finger on a Roulette iPad app, but it's often extremely impractical to be betting on spots all over a real-world table, since you may have to reach over other people. These systems also often require betting odd amounts if there's any Martingale-style "doubling-up" involved when you lose.

But if you can consolidate your bets using the Distributive Property of Roulette Betting, you can often save time and prevent yourself from encroaching awkwardly into other people's personal space. On a Roulette table, reach-arounds are a bad thing.

A few days ago, The CEG Dealer School reviewed a Roulette system named "Diamond Heist", where they made 6 corner bets that covered the entire first two columns. For the purposes of this post, we're going to ignore the other bets involved in this system.

So, there is $30 covering the first two columns evenly (with a betting density of $1.25 per spot). This is the perfect candidate for consolidation! Since there's no "Double Column" bet, we can instead place $15 on each of the first two column bets.

We've saved ourselves some time and we've eliminated the awkward "reach-around"! This is what the Distributive Property of Roulette Betting is all about!
