If you're the type of person who likes to spread their bets around the Roulette table, you should know about the Distributive Property of Roulette Bets. It can save you a lot of time and hassle if your bets are consolidated in certain areas.

What is the Distributive Property of Roulette Bets?

The Distributive Property of Roulette states

Betting $X on an n-number bet is equivalent to betting \($X \over n\) each the covered numbers straight-up.

In other words

\({$X \times Payout_n} = {$X \over n} \times Payout_1 \)

Where \(Payout_n\) is the payout multiplier for an n-number bet

So betting $6 on the 6-number bet, is equivalent to betting $1 on each of the numbers individually.

With the single $6 Double Street bet, we win 5-to-1, for a net win of $30.

With the six $1 Straight-up bets, we will lose $5, but win $35 on the straight-up bet, for a net win of $30.

So in both scenarios, we lose $6 when we miss and win $30 when we hit.

And this Distributive Property of Roulette Bets works with any combination of bets where the bets are all uniform. So you can consolidate three straight-up bets into a 3 number Street bet. And you can consolidate four straight-up bets into one 4 number Corner bet. You can even consolidate two Street bets into a single Double Street bet.

And this works for all bets with the exception of the 5 number (Basket) bet, which is a bet on the numbers , , , , and .

What is this Sorcery?

This all magically works out because all Roulette Bets would be "fair" (i.e. There would be no House Edge) if There were no Zeroes on the wheel. So as long as your density of bets is consistent (the amount of money bet evenly across numbers), then the payout should be identical.

And we can prove this mathematcially. The payout for every bet in Roulette (besides the 5 number "Basket" bet) can be calculated by using the formula:

$$ { Payout_{n-number-bet} } = { {36 - n} \over n } $$

So an $n wager on an n-number bet can be calculated as

\begin{align} n \times { Payout_n } & = { n \times { {36 - n} \over n } } \\ \\ & = 36 - n \\ \\ & = 36 - 1 - n + 1 \\ \\ & = { {36 - 1} \over 1 } - (n - 1) \\ \\ & = { Payout_1 } - (n - 1) \\ \end{align}

Which is the win from a single $1 straight-up bet minus the (n-1) losses from the losing straight-up bets.

For example, if you bet $1 on 12 numbers straight-up, then one of those bets will win $35 and 11 of those bets will lose, for a net win of $24, which is what your payout would be for betting $12 on a dozen or a column.

\begin{align} $12 \times { Payout_{12} } & = { Payout_1 } - (12 - 1) \\ \\ $12 \times 2 & = $35 - $11 \\ \\ $24 & = $24 \\ \end{align}

Terminology

To tell you the truth, I've never heard of the Distributive Property of Roulette Betting. In fact, I just made the term up. That doesn't make it any less valid, though. It works and I've proven that it works, so I suppose that gives me the right to name it whatever I want.

So, with that being said, let me introduce you to some of the other terms involved in this property.

Consolidation

Consolidation is "rolling-up" individual bets into a single bet with the same win/loss characteristics. A typical consolidation is taking n number of $X bets into a single $Nx multi-number bet.

Distribution

Distribution or Decomposition is taking a multi-number bet and breaking it down into smaller bets, often a series of "straight-up" single-number bets.

It's also possible to decompose a multi-number bet into smaller multi-number bets. For example, a $10 double-street bet can be broken down into two $5 street bets.

Density

Density or Betting Density is the distributed value per spot once a consolidated bet has been fully distributed. You can also think of this as the equity per spot.

In order for two betting scenarios to be equivalent, their betting density must be equal for the covered area. For example, you can bet $6 on the - Double Street, but that would have a different density compared to betting $3 on the , , , Corner Bet in conjunction with a $3 , Split Bet. This implies that the two betting schemes are not equivalent and will pay out differently when the numbers hit.
