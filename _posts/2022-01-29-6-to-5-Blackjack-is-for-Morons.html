The House Edge of 6-to-5

You can calculate the cost of playing 6-to-5 Blackjack pretty easily. To start, you need to know how frequently you can expect a starting hand of 21 and I promise it's easy to remember (You'll be quizzed on this later).

On average, you will receive a Blackjack every 21 hands.

So estimating the cost of this rule variant becomes pretty easy. Multiply the probability of having a Blackjack (\(1 \over 21\)) by the probability of the dealer not having a Blackjack (\(1 - {1 \over 21}\) or \(20 \over 21\)) and the change in your payout, which is 0.3 of your initial bet (60% of your half-bet bonus) and you get:

\begin{align} {1 \over 21} \times {20 \over 21} \times -0.3 & \approx -0.0136054 \\ & \approx \color{red}{-1.36054\%} \end{align}

That's a very good approximation for the cost of this rule variant! When you look at the actual cost of the rule, it comes very close:
