Around the year 2006, an option for "Running it Twice" started to become popular on televised Poker cash games. It became a pretty popular on the show High Stakes Poker on GSN and several other cash game shows.

But what affect does running it once, twice, or even 44 times do to your expected result? How does it affect your variance?

What is "Running it Twice"?

Two players can agree to "run it twice" when one of the players is all-in and there are still more cards to come. When the players agree to "run it twice", they are agreeing to complete the hand with two sets of outcomes. If there is one card left to be dealt, then two cards will be dealt, each card acting as a the final community card for two separate outcomes. The winner of each outcome will be entited to a half of the total pot.

If you win both times, you win the entire pot. If you lose both times, you lose the entire pot. If you win one and lose the other, then the pot is divided in half and distributed to the two players.

What about Running it 3 or 4 or even 44 Times?

You can "run it" multiple times. Each time you run it, the pot shares become smaller and smaller, and it becomes more likely that the underdog will get at least some of his money back.

Why can't you Run it Twice in a Tournament?

The option to run it multiple times usually only applies to cash games. You probably won't have this option available in a tournament for two big reasons.

It's disruptive

Deciding how many times to run it takes time.
Dealing extra turns and extra rivers takes time.
Splitting the pot and even figuring out who won each pot takes time

And time is often your enemy when blind levels increase every couple of minutes.

It opens the door for collusion

If two players are soft-playing each other because they are friends or if they both have a financial interest in the other winning (i.e. if they are partially staking each other), then it's in their best interest to keep each other in the tournament by winning a split pot as opposed to being eliminated.

Even though it's possible to collude without running it twice, keeping your friends afloat becomes pretty easy when you give him multiple opportunities to spike his one-outer.

Worst of all, there's no reason why anyone in the tournament would want a player to avoid possible elimination. The only person who benefits from winning part of a split pot would be the player who is all-in.

How does Running it Multiple Times affect Your EV?

It doesn't.

Your Expected Value will be exactly the same as if you just ran it once, twice, or even 44 times.

How does Running it Multiple Times affect Your Variance?

The only statistical change that results from multiple run-outs is a lowering of variance. The more turns and rivers you see, the more your results will resemble the expected result and the lower the fluctuation to your bankroll.

Calculating the Results of Running it N Times

I think most seasoned poker players are already aware that running it multiple times results in zero change to your EV. But I've often heard noobs (incorrectly) say that running it multiple times is bad for the favorite in the situation.

1-outer on the River

Let's take a look at an extreme situation.

I think the best way to illustrate that the EV remains the same is to look at the case where the underdog has a one-outer with just the River to come.
