Around the year 2006, an option for "Running it Twice" started to become popular on televised Poker cash games. It became a pretty popular on the show High Stakes Poker on GSN and several other cash game shows.

What is "Running it Twice"?

The decision to "run it twice" is made when there is an all-in situation involving only two players. When the players agree to "run it twice", they are agreeing to complete the hand with two sets of outcomes. If there is one card left to be dealt, then two cards will be dealt, each card acting as a the final community card for two separate outcomes. The winner of each outcome will be entited to a share of the total pot.

If you win both times, you win the entire pot. If you lose both times, you lose the entire pot. If you win one and lose the other, then the pot is divided in half and distributed to the two players.
