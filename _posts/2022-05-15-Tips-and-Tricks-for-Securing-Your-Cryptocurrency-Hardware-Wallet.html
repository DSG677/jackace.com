What's the best way of keeping your cryptocurrency wallet secure? There are three major steps that you should be taking in keeping your crypto safe.

  1. Create and Secure a 24-Word Seed Phrase
  2. Add and Secure a "25th" Word to your Seed Phrase
  3. Use and Secure your PIN

Create and SECURE a 24-Word Seed Phrase

The first step in securing your hardware wallet is to create a secure seed phrase. Even though shorter phrases are available, use the 24 word option, which is (obviously) the most secure.

Seed phrases used with hardware wallets are based on the BIP-39 specificiation, so they are meant to be interchangable amongst compatible hardware wallets.

How to Secure your Seed Phrase

NEVER store your Seed Phrase electronically in ANY FORM. This means you need to write it down on paper, or better yet, you should scribe it on metal. This copy of your Seed Phrase should be stored in a home safe or a safety deposit box.

This also means that you should not take a picture of it! Digital photos often end up on a Cloud server (either purposely or accidentally), where they can be scanned with OCR software and easily checked for words on the seed phrase list.

You should avoid recording your Seed Phrase in any of the following formats:

  • Any digital text file or word processing file
  • In a Password Manager like LastPass or 1Password
  • A photo of any format - digital or even print

Exposure

Any person who has access to your Seed Phrase can recreate your wallet without requiring physical access to your hardware wallet AND without knowing your PIN. They can access all the crypto in your (main) wallet. This is why protecting your Seed Phrase is the most important step in securing your hardware wallet.

Phishing scams that get crypto holders to volunteer their seed phrase are amongst the most common of crypto scams and have resulted in the forfeiture of millions of dollars worth of crypto. You need to ensure you keep your seed phrase 100% offline.

Add a "25th" Word to your Seed Phrase

The major hardware wallets all support a "25th" word. This can be used to create a "hidden" wallet (or multiple hidden wallets, if you desire).

The 25th word protects your crypto from falling into the wrong hands if someone gets a hold of your Seed Phrase list. If you were to keep that list in a safe deposit box, any banker with the authority to force open boxes (which can happen when you lose the key or if law enforcement tells them to).

If there is a "reasonable" suspicion that illegal items may be in a safe deposit box, law enforcement officials can obtain a court order to freeze the owner's access to it. In criminal cases investigators can force the box open and seize its contents. They don't even need to seize the contents - they might just snap a photo of your Seed Phrase in the box and restore your wallet when they got off work.

The 25th word protects you in situations like this.

How to Secure your 25th Word

Your 25th word can be stored digitally using a password manager like LastPass or 1Password. NEVER STORE YOUR 25TH WORD WITH YOUR SEED PHRASE!

Exposure

If someone is able to obtain your 25th word, it's pretty worthless unless they also know the entire seed phrase. This is why you should never store your 25th word with your offline seed phrase.

Add a PIN to your Hardware Wallet

Every hardware wallet requires a PIN. You should choose a 6-digit PIN (or longer if possible) that you must enter to access coins in your crypto hardware wallet.

You may need to enter your PIN several times when performing operations with your wallet.

How to Secure your PIN

Your 25th word can also be stored digitally using a password manager like LastPass or 1Password. You should NOT write down your PIN.

Exposure

Anyone who has physical access to your active hardware wallet can access and remove your funds if they know your PIN. This is why it should be safe to keep it stored in a cloud-based password manager. Hackers from half-way around the world wouldn't be a threat to being able to connect your hardware wallet to their USB port, so storing your PIN in the cloud is just fine.

Advanced Tips for Securing Your Hardware Wallet

Use a Decoy (Non-Hidden) Wallet

If you use a 25th word, then if someone found your full 24-Word Seed Phrase, then if a theif were to recover your wallet, they would see it would be empty. If they knew about 25th word security, they might go through the efforts of trying to find out the 25th word.

Imagine a scenario where home invaders (who know you have crypto) tie you up and break open your safe. In your safe, they find a sheet of paper with your 24-Word phrase. Being knowledgable about crypto, they restore the phrase and find a wallet with no crypto.

Since they know your Seed Phrase is valid, they might use physical force to get you to surrender the 25th word, which would allow them to access all your coin.

But if you put a fraction of your crypto (1% to 5%) in the main wallet associated with your 24-Word Seed Phrase, then when they recover the wallet, they may believe they found your main stash. They may not go through the extra effort of trying to find your 25th word if they think they already hit the jackpot.

Use a Decoy PIN

You might write down a fake or invalid PIN somewhere near your physical hardware wallet. If you do this, write down the longest number that is still valid as a PIN length for your device. And make sure you make a few of the numbers ambiguous ("Is that a '1' or a '7'? Is that a '6' or a '0'?"). Most hardware wallets make you wait a while between PIN entries, getting exponentially longer between unsuccessful attempts. Also, there is usually an upper limit to attempts, with the device wipiing itself when the user makes too many bad attempts in a row.
