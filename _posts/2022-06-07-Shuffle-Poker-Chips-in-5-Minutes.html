What do you need to Shuffle Poker Chips?

To learn how to shuffle poker chips, you need three things:

  • The proper materials
  • Preparation
  • Proper technique

Most of the tutorials I've seen only talk about technique. That's usually fine if you're planning on practicing at a casino, but most people will be learning at home.

Materials

There are two material items you need to shuffle poker chips:

  • Quality Poker Chips
  • A Padded Surface

Quality Poker Chips

This is the obvious item, but it helps if you use chips that have a bit of wear and tear to them. It really helps.

Metal Dollar Tokens

If you can get your hands on 8 of the old-school Vegas slot tokens, that is the absolute easiest type of chip you can start with. They are usually well weathered and are easy to shuffle. You basically just need to push them together and they'll shuffle themselves. It is a great way to get the feel of how the chips should be coming together and interleaving.

Real Casino Chips

If you can get 8 non-slippery casino chips, they work best. Since they've presumably been used in a casino, they probably have natural wear-and-tear, which is great for learning how to shuffle.

Standard "Home Game" Poker Chips

And the poker chips commonly used in "home games" and available for relatively cheap on Amazon work well, too. Just make sure you break them in using techniques in the preparation part of this post.

Preparation

There are three main steps in preparing to shuffle chips.

  • Wash the chips
  • Wash your hands
  • Prepare your area

This is an often overlooked step in learning chip shuffling. Any oil in your hands is going to make learning a lot more difficult. Also, new poker chips will often have a fine powder on them, which makes precise handling of the chips hard.

So wash your hands well before you start practicing. I would also have some hand wipes to give your hands a quick rinse if you build up any oil in your hands while practicing. Trust me, this is going to help you a lot more than you might realize.

And wipe the chips down, as well. New home game chips will have a fine dust on them that makes pushing them together more difficult. Old casino chips will often have oil on them from being man-handled for so long.

Technique

You should learn how to shuffle chips in the following order:

  • Shuffle 2+2 Metal Dollar Slugs (if available)
  • Shuffle 4+4 Metal Dollar Slugs (if available)
  • Shuffle 2+2 Poker Chips
  • Shuffle 4+4 Poker Chips
  • Shuffle 8+8 Poker Chips
  • Shuffle 4+4 Chips (Hard Mode)
  • Shuffle 8+8 Chips (Hard Mode)
  • BONUS: Repeat all these steps for your non-dominant hand (I did for this post!)

Shuffling Metal Dollar Slugs

If you can get a hold of some old school metal dollar slugs that casinos used to use in slot machines, they are excellent for getting a feel for the shuffle.

The edges are usually well-worn and you can basically just squeeze the tokens together and they will magically interleave.
